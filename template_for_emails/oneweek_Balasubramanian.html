NOVEMBER 16th, at 14h30 (BRT)

Vijay Balasubramanian

Professor of Physics at the University of Pennsylvania, where received the Ira H. Abrams Memorial Award for Distinguished Teaching. Currently, he is visiting Professor at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VuB) in Belgium and at the CUNY Graduate Center. He will be talking about his research and advances on Biophysics & Neuroscience.

His research: Many of the most interesting functions and behaviors realized by the brain emerge from the collective activity of very large numbers of neurons of many functional types interacting in complex networks. I seek to establish the principles underlying the structural and functional organization of such circuits in the brain, specifically: (1) How large-scale behaviors emerge from the interaction of many individual elements, (2) What the constraints on neural circuit architecture are, (3) How network functions can be impaired or improved by changes to the architecture. Read more details of his research on his homepage. Further information and publications are available at his lab website.
